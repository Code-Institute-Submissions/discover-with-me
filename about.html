Monuments

The most famous places to visit in my city are:
Gothic Cathedral of St. Stanislaus and St. Wenceslaus from the 14th century, which has the highest tower in Silesia, standing around 101 meters tall;
Evangelical Church of Peace, which is registered on the UNESCO World Culture Heritage List. It was built in one-year 1656–57, and is made from wood, clay, sand and straw. No metal nails were used to build it. Everybody assumed this church would survive only around 50 years. Now, is the proud society of protestants.
16th-century Town Hall has been renovated numerous times and combines Gothic, Renaissance, and Baroque architectural elements with The Theatre and The Museum of Old Commerce in Świdnica.
The Market Squere,
The Baroque Church of St. Joseph and new opened Diocesan Museum.
The Church of St. Christopher are from the same era, The Chapel of St. Barbara.
And my beloved Public Library which is located in renovated The Cistercian Abbots’ Palace, with baroque gantry and interesting roofed patio.

Around the city

In close area you can see more interesting places. My favorite is Castle Książ. I love this place, it is amazing, historical and beautiful. It’s the largest castle in Lower Silesia and third largest castle in Poland. Each year there is a famous Flower Festival that is extremely popular amongst tourists. It is a great opportunity to visit the castle’s underground, rooms and gardens.
Close by in Ciernie you can see large ruins of Widow’s Palace, which hold a very sad, romantic story.
• In Witoszów Dolny you can see The Weapons Museum. Among the items donated by the army and other collectors we can admire tanks, airplanes, several rifles and pistols as well as other types of guns and white weapons, uniforms and cars and motorcycles. • In Mokszeszów there is an old, amazing castle built in 1880-3 by the order of The Maltese Knightswhich. In the years 1938-1945, the castle became a tenement house. At that time, The "Mothers' House" was run (for women with pure Aryan blood), which were imported from all over Germany. From the 1950s, there was a school in the castle. Now, it is a private property. • In Jaworzyna Śląska you can see The Museum of Indrusty and Railway in Silesia. • I like Zagórze Śląskie there is a dam on the river, which creates a lake located between mountains with one of the most beautiful views in my area. Castle Grodno, which is located on the top of one of the mountains. • Museum Gross-Rossen (of the former Nazi Concentration camp) is also a short drive away. • Very interesting place to visit is Ślęża Mountain with Tourist House, Observations Tower and Church dedicated to the Visitation of the Blessed Virgin Mary on the top. This is the highest the peak of the Ślęża Massif.

People

In my city there were and are famous people. Someone of them are living in the city, some around in close area. I want to introduce you only few of them. • For me the most interesting person is Emil Kerbs. It is a man, who was fluent in 12 languages on his 17 birthday. By the time he died, in 1930, he learned 120 languages and was fluent in 68 other languages (it is proven). • Maria Kunitz (1604-1664) – she was astronomer. She studied the history of medicine and mathematics, she knew 7 languages. • Anna Świdnicka niece Bolka II. Wife Charles IV of Luxembourg, King of Bohemia and Roman Emperor of the German nation. Mother of Wenceslas IV, the king of Czech and German. • From Świdnica is Marek Michalak, who was the ombudsman for children’s rights in Poland until last December. As the ombudsman of children's rights, he appointed the Family Law Codification Commission at his office (which, among other things, proposed introducing a child's law to both parents). He was also the initiator of the launch of a free nationwide line of the Children's Phone of Confidence RPD • Anna Barańska she is a volleyball player, who represents Poland. Medalist of the World Championships and European Championships (2001 - silver medal of the European Championship and brown cadets, 2003 - gold medal of the European Championship, 2004 - bronze medal of the Junior World Championships). • Paweł Fajdek (born June 4, 1989 in Świebodzice) - Polish athlete specializing in hammer throwing, the youngest in history gold medalist of the World Cup in this competition. Raised in Żarów. • From a close by area comes Krzysztof Piątek. A Polish footballer, who is a big sensation at the moment as he is a striker at the Italian club A.C. Milan and the Polish national team. Participant of the U-21 European Championship 2017. He played in a friendly match against Ireland on 11 September 2018. • Bartosz Kurek was also born close by. He is a famous volleyball player, who won Volleyball World Cup last year. He was named the best volleyball player in Europe as well as the best Polish athlete of the year 2018. • Kama Karst born in Świdnica, painter, artist, she paints fantastic pictures and horses. • Jarek and Przemek Tarłowscy they create and still provide website about Świdnica, where you can read about history our town and see historical photos. • Piotr Snopczyński Instructor, climber, photographer, sportsman, height specialist, educator. He is the originator of the "Mountain Days" of Świdnica, which he organizes together with the Świdnica Cultural Center.
